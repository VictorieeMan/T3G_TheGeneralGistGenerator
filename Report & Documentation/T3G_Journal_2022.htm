Introduction & stated goal

Is it possible to find a method of programmatically generate the gist of a comment section or the replies of a survey? This project will explore that line of questioning and aim to develop a method, capable of summarizing human written texts in a meaningful manner.

Applications that inspire the direction of this project:

·         Summarizing large quantities of replies to the same question in a survey, to understand the general gist of what the respondents conveyed. (Similar to the manual process of asking someone read hundreds or even thousands of replies to summarize the general feedback.)

·         Summarizing all the comments of for instance a YouTube video, to get the general “review” / feedback from the audience. YouTube videos with many views often get more comments than possible for one person to read.

When we use the word “gist” in this project we refer to the Oxford dictionary’s definition of the phrase: https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/gist

gist (of something) the main or general meaning of a piece of writing, a speech or a conversation.

In application we are as seen in the examples above, mainly interested in sets of shorter texts of large quantities that all refer to responding to the same question and or commenting the same thing. And hence, large quantities of text that in some sense is guided in the same direction of meaning by a mutual target.

Earlier work in this field

According to feedback from Erik Aas, working at Google in Zürich, the most similar applications to our problem have had this historical development of technique: Markov generator (1980) --> RNN (2010) --> LSTM (2015) --> Transformers (2018) [The well known GPT3 for instance].

Even tough there are some solutions out there that are akin to what we are looking for, we haven’t at the start of this project found anything that’s an exact fit to what we are looking for. An easy applicable solution specialized in summarizing similar entries.

In our first approach we are interested in taking a look at a combination of applying the Markov generator approach with a sentimental analysis, that keeps the sentimental average of the generated summary close to the average of the whole examined set.
